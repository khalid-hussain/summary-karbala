
# Two Prophecies

<!-- TODO: Abdul Hakeem: Consider putting information about these all in a single place -->

There are two remarkable prophecies of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
that are closely connected to the lives of al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. One concerns the reconciliation of the Muslims through
al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, while the other concerns the martyrdom of
al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

## The Prophecy Concerning al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\HEADER}

Following the martyrdom of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} in 40 AH, the
Muslims pledged allegiance to al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} as Khalīfah. At the
same time, Muʿāwiyah ibn Abī Sufyān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was recognized as ruler
in Syria and the territories under his authority. Seeking to prevent further
bloodshed among the Muslims, al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} entered into
negotiations with Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and eventually concluded a peace
treaty in 41 AH. Both al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} thereafter
pledged allegiance to Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and the Muslims united
under a single ruler and the divisions that had persisted since the martyrdom of
ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} largely came to an end. This year became known as
ʿĀm al-Jamāʿah (The Year of Unity) because the divisions that had afflicted the
Ummah were largely brought to an end. (_Tārīkh al-Ṭabarī_)

The Companions \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} who had remained neutral during the conflict
between the two sides also pledged their allegiance to Muʿāwiyah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} at this time, bringing about a broad consensus and unity
within the Ummah. Al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} thereafter withdrew from
political affairs and devoted himself to worship and teaching until his passing
in Madīnah in 49 AH at approximately forty-seven years of age. (_Al-Iṣābah_)

This historic reconciliation fulfilled the prophecy of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Abū Bakrah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I saw the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} upon the pulpit while al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} was beside him. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would look at
the people and then at al-Ḥasan and say, ‘Indeed this son of mine is a leader
(Sayyid), and perhaps Allāh will reconcile through him two great groups of
Muslims.’” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

The scholars also point out that al-Ḥasan's six-month caliphate completed
another prophecy of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, “The Caliphate
after me will last for thirty years, then there will be kingship thereafter.”
(_Sunan Abī Dāwūd_; _Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī_)

When the caliphates of Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, ʿUthmān, and ʿAlī \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}
are combined, they amount to approximately twenty-nine and a half years. The
six-month caliphate of al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} completes the thirty years
mentioned by the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. For this reason, the scholars of
Ahl al-Sunnah regard al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} as the fifth and final
al-Khalīfah al-Rāshid (Rightly-Guided Caliph). (_Al-Bidāyah wa al-Nihāyah_)

An interesting conversation is recorded by Imam Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr
\hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}. During the final illness of al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, he addressed his brother al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and
reflected upon the history of the Khilāfah after the passing of the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. He said,

> O my dear brother, when the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} passed away,
> our father desired this matter (the Khilāfah), but Allāh turned it away from
> him, and Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} became the Khalīfah. When Abū Bakr
> \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} died, he desired it again, but it was turned to ʿUmar
> \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Then when ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was dying, he made it a
> consultation between six men, and our father was one of them. He was certain
> that it would not escape him that time, yet it was turned away from him and
> given to ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Then when ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was
> killed, our father became Khalīfah, but disagreement arose, and he had to fight
> with the sword. Thus, the Khilāfah did not come to him in a pure and uncontested
> manner, and he endured much hardship because of it. Therefore, by Allāh, I do
> not think that Allāh will combine for us, the Ahl al-Bayt, both Prophethood and
> the Khilāfah. (_Al-Istīʿāb fī Maʿrifat al-Aṣḥāb_)

## The Prophecy Concerning al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\HEADER}

Multiple narrations indicate that Allāh informed the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} of the future martyrdom of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.
Imam Aḥmad \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} records on the authority of Anas ibn Mālik
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} that the Angel of Rain sought permission from his Lord to
visit the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, and permission was granted to
him. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to Umm Salamah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY},
“Guard the door for us and do not allow anyone to enter.”

While they were together, al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} came and sought
permission to enter. Umm Salamah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} attempted to stop him, but
he slipped past her and entered. He then began climbing upon the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, sitting upon his back and shoulders as young children
would do. The angel then asked, “Do you love him?”

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, “Yes.” The angel said,
“Indeed, your Ummah will kill him. If you wish, I can show you the land where he
will be killed.” The angel then struck the earth with his hand and produced some
red clay. Umm Salamah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} took the clay and tied it in her
garment.

Thābit al-Bunānī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}, one of the sub-narrators, said, “It has
reached us that it was the land of Karbalāʾ.” (_Musnad Aḥmad_)

Likewise, Imam Aḥmad \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} narrates through Wakīʿ, from ʿAbdullāh
ibn Saʿīd, from his father, from either ʿĀʾishah or Umm Salamah
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} that the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “An
angel visited me today who had never visited me before. He said to me,
‘Indeed, this son of yours, al-Ḥusayn, will be killed. If you wish, I can show
you the soil of the land in which he will be killed.’”

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then said that the angel brought forth some red
soil from that land. (_Musnad Aḥmad_)

The individual chains of these narrations vary in strength, and some contain
weaknesses. However, many scholars of Ḥadīth, including al-Ḥākim, al-Dhahabī,
Ibn Kathīr, and others \hon{\M_THM}{\BODY}, have noted that the numerous reports
on this subject strengthen one another. Taken collectively, they establish that
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was informed of the future martyrdom of his
beloved grandson al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and that this tragic event would
occur in the land of Karbalāʾ. (_Dalāʾil al-Nubuwwah_)

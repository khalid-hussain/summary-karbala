
# Abū Bakr and ʿUmar\'s Love for the Ahl al-Bayt

One of the greatest misconceptions spread by the Shia is that Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} were enemies of the Ahl al-Bayt. The historical reality
becomes even clearer when we examine the relationship from the other side. Not
only did the Ahl al-Bayt love, honor, and praise Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, but Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} likewise
demonstrated immense love, reverence, and respect for the family of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} throughout their lives.

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} relates that Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Honor Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} by honoring his
household.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} also said, “By the One in whose Hand is my soul,
maintaining ties with the relatives of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
is more beloved to me than maintaining ties with my own relatives.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

ʿUqbah ibn al-Ḥārith \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated, “I saw Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} carrying al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} on his
shoulders and saying, ‘May my father be sacrificed for you! You resemble the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and not ʿAlī.’ ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was standing
nearby laughing.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_) This occurred only a short time after the
passing of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

When Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} fell ill during her final illness, Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} visited her and said, “By Allāh, I never abandoned my
wealth, my family, my tribe, or my relatives except seeking the pleasure of
Allāh, the pleasure of His Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, and the pleasure of the
Ahl al-Bayt.” He continued speaking kindly to her until she became pleased with
him. (_Al-Bidāyah wa al-Nihāyah_)

Likewise, Abū Bakr's wife, Asmāʾ bint ʿUmays \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, personally
attended to Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} during her final illness and remained
with her until she passed away. She was also among those responsible for washing
and shrouding her.

ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} once visited Fāṭimah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} and said, “O Fāṭimah! By Allāh, I have not seen anyone more
beloved to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} than you. And by Allāh,
after your father, there is no one more beloved to me than you.” (_Al-Mustadrak_)

When Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} passed away, Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} attended her funeral prayer. Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
offered ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} the opportunity to lead the prayer. ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} declined, saying, “How can I lead the prayer whilst you are
present, O Khalīfah of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}?” Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} then stepped forward and led the funeral prayer. (_Al-Ṭabaqāt
al-Kubrá_)

Following the conquest of al-Ḥīrah, Khālid ibn al-Walīd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} sent
valuable spoils to Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, including an expensive shawl
and one thousand dirhams. Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} gifted the shawl to
al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. (_Futūḥ al-Buldān_)

When the state registers were established and stipends allocated during the
Khilāfah of ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, he placed al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} amongst those who had participated in Badr because of their
relationship to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Each of them was
allocated fifty thousand dirhams. (_Tārīkh Dimashq_)

Jābir \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated that after marrying Umm Kulthūm bint ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} addressed the
people and said, “Will you not congratulate me? I heard the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} say, ‘Every lineage and relationship will be cut off on the
Day of Judgment except my lineage and my relationship.’” (_Al-Muʿjam al-Awsaṭ_)

Umm Kulthūm bint ʿAlī \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} married ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} in
17 AH with a _mahr_ of forty thousand dirhams. Through this marriage, ʿUmar
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} became the son-in-law of ʿAlī and Fāṭimah
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} became his
grandfather-in-law. Al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} became his
brothers-in-law, and their children became connected through ties of kinship.

Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥanafīyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “One day ʿUmar
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} entered whilst I was with my sister Umm Kulthūm
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}. He embraced me and then said to her, ‘Treat him with
sweets.’” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

Among these marriages was the marriage of ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} to Asmāʾ bint
ʿUmays \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, the widow of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.
Al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} married Ḥafṣah bint ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Abī
Bakr. Muḥammad al-Bāqir \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} married Umm Farwah bint al-Qāsim ibn
Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr, and from this union was born Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq
\hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}. Likewise, Umm Kulthūm bint ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} married
ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, whilst descendants of al-Ḥasan and
al-Ḥusayn married into the families of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān, al-Zubayr ibn
al-ʿAwwām, and other noble Companions \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}. Such marriages are
not the actions of families consumed by hatred and hostility toward one another.

The descendants of the Ahl al-Bayt repeatedly named their children Abū Bakr,
ʿUmar, ʿUthmān, and ʿĀʾishah. This occurred so frequently that these names
appear throughout the genealogical works of both Sunni and Shia historians.
Among those who bore these names were several descendants of al-Ḥasan and
al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, including men who were martyred at Karbalāʾ
itself.

Among them were Abū Bakr ibn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and Abū Bakr
ibn al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, both of whom were martyred at
Karbalāʾ. Likewise, ʿUmar al-Aṭraf ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, ʿUmar ibn
al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and ʿUmar ibn al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
carried the name of ʿUmar. ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} also named one of his sons
ʿUthmān ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, who was likewise martyred at Karbalāʾ.
Among the women of the Ahl al-Bayt were several named ʿĀʾishah, including
ʿĀʾishah bint Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq and ʿĀʾishah bint Mūsá al-Kāẓim
\hon{\M_THM}{\BODY}.

These reports are only a small sample of the affection, respect, and family ties
that existed between the Ṣaḥābah and the Ahl al-Bayt. The historical record
simply does not support the claim that Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}
harbored hatred towards the family of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Rather, the opposite is abundantly clear: they loved,
honored, supported, and maintained close relations with the Ahl al-Bayt
throughout their lives.

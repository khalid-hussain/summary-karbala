
# Shabath ibn Ribʿī: The Man Who Changed Sides at Every Crossroad

Few individuals better embody the political chaos, opportunism, and treachery
that characterized Kūfah during the first century of Islam than Shabath ibn
Ribʿī al-Tamīmī. His life reads less like the biography of a single man and more
like a catalog of nearly every major _fitnah_ that afflicted the early Muslim
community.

Al-Ḥāfiẓ Ibn Ḥajar \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} describes him as one of those who lived
during both the Jāhilīyah and Islamic eras. He initially followed Sajāḥ bint
al-Ḥārith, the notorious false prophetess who emerged after the death of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. After her movement collapsed, Shabath
returned to Islam. Yet this would be only the first of many dramatic shifts in
allegiance that marked his life. (_Taqrīb al-Tahdhīb_)

During the Khilāfah of ʿUthmān ibn ʿAffān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, Shabath became
involved in the unrest and rebellion that eventually culminated in the martyrdom
of the third Khalīfah. When the Ummah later rallied behind ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, Shabath joined his camp and fought under his banner.
However, his loyalty proved short-lived. Following the arbitration at Ṣiffīn, he
defected to the Khawārij and became one of those who opposed ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. When the Khawārij movement began to fracture, he abandoned
them as well.

Had his story ended there, it would already have been an insane life. But wait,
there is more.

Like many of the leading figures of Kūfah, Shabath was among those who wrote
letters and encouraged al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} to come to Kūfah.
The Kūfans flooded al-Ḥusayn with letters promising support, pledging
allegiance, and assuring him that tens of thousands stood ready to fight on his
behalf. Yet when the moment of truth arrived, and Ibn Ziyād's threats began,
many of these same people abandoned their promises. Among them was Shabath ibn
Ribʿī. Instead of standing beside the grandson of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, he eventually found himself amongst the Kūfan ranks that
opposed him. Thus, a man who had helped create the circumstances that drew
al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} to Kūfah was present during the very events that
led to his martyrdom.

One would think that witnessing such a catastrophe might have changed him.
Instead, after the death of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, Shabath once again
changed course.

When al-Mukhtār ibn Abī ʿUbayd al-Thaqafī launched his movement in Kūfah under
the slogan of avenging the blood of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, Shabath
joined him and rose to a position of influence. Al-Ḥāfiẓ Ibn Ḥajar
\hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} mentions that he even served amongst the leadership of Kūfah
and held the position of chief of police. Yet, true to form, his allegiance did
not last. Before long, he abandoned al-Mukhtār and joined those fighting against
him, ultimately contributing to the destruction of the very movement he had once
supported. (_Taqrīb al-Tahdhīb_)

Al-Ziriklī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} summarized his extraordinary career by describing
him as one of the leading figures of Kūfah in his era. He notes that Shabath
followed Sajāḥ, returned to Islam, participated in the rebellion against ʿUthmān
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, fought alongside ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, opposed him with
the Khawārij, fought against al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, joined al-Mukhtār,
and then fought against al-Mukhtār with great determination. (_Al-Aʿlām_)

Throughout his life, he moved from one camp to another, from one cause to
another, and from one allegiance to another. In a single individual, one sees
the very disease that consumed Kūfah: lofty words, passionate claims, and solemn
pledges, followed by abandonment when sacrifice was required. The road to
Karbalāʾ was paved not only by the swords of Ibn Ziyād's men, but also by the
failure of those who had promised to stand with al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

Many people know the truth, praise the truth, and even call others to the truth,
yet fail when the moment comes to stand for it. May Allāh protect us from
misguidance after guidance, from betrayal after pledges, and from dying upon
anything other than truth and steadfastness.


# The Ahl al-Bayt on Abū Bakr and ʿUmar

One of the greatest misconceptions propagated during Muḥarram is that the Ahl
al-Bayt and the senior Companions of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
were adversaries. The historical reality is the exact opposite. Do you wish to
know the status of these two men? Look at where they are buried today and who
their neighbor is. The statements of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib, his children, his
grandchildren, and the leading figures of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
household are filled with praise, love, respect, and recognition of the virtue
of Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq and ʿUmar al-Fārūq \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}.

There is even a rare occurrence where ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated a
_ḥadīth_ from the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} through Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Indeed, I am a man who,
when I hear a _ḥadīth_ from the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, Allāh
causes me to benefit from it as much as He wills. When one of his Companions
narrates a _ḥadīth_ to me, I ask him to swear an oath regarding it, and when he
swears, I believe him. Abū Bakr narrated to me---and Abū Bakr spoke the
truth---that he heard the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} say, ‘There is
no servant who commits a sin, then performs _wuḍūʾ_ well, prays two _rakʿahs_, and
seeks forgiveness from Allāh except that Allāh forgives him.’”

Thereafter he recited,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱلَّذِينَ إِذَا فَعَلُواْ فَٰحِشَةً أَوۡ ظَلَمُوٓاْ أَنفُسَهُمۡ ذَكَرُواْ ٱللَّهَ فَٱسۡتَغۡفَرُواْ لِذُنُوبِهِمۡ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And those who, when they commit an indecency or wrong themselves, remember Allāh
and seek forgiveness for their sins... (Āl ʿImrān, Q3:135; _Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī_)
\AYAH_END

Notice the words of ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} himself, “Abū Bakr spoke the
truth.”

Our mother ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} narrated that during the Wars of
Apostasy, Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} mounted his animal with his sword
unsheathed and headed towards Dhū al-Qiṣṣah. Upon seeing this, ʿAlī ibn Abī
Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} hurried after him, grasped the reins of his mount and
said, “Where are you going, O Khalīfah of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}? I will say to you exactly what the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to you on the Day of Uḥud, ‘Sheathe your sword and do
not cause us to suffer through losing you.’ By Allāh, if we are afflicted by
your loss today, Islām will never prosper thereafter.” (_Al-Bidāyah wa
al-Nihāyah_)

Jaʿfar ibn Muḥammad narrated from his father that a man once asked ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} regarding a sermon in which he supplicated, “O Allāh,
rectify us as You rectified the al-Khulafāʾ al-Rāshidūn before us.” The man
asked, “Who were those Khulafāʾ?”

With tears in his eyes, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, “They were my beloved
ones, your two uncles: Abū Bakr and ʿUmar. They were leaders from Quraysh and
exemplars of guidance. They were followed after the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Whoever followed their path was protected, whoever emulated
them was guided to the Straight Path, and whoever held firmly to their way was
from the Party of Allāh. Verily, the Party of Allāh are the successful ones.”
(_Ḥilyat al-Awliyāʾ_)

ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} also declared from the pulpit, “Indeed, Abū Bakr was
tender-hearted and constantly turned back to Allāh. Indeed, ʿUmar was sincere
with Allāh, so Allāh treated him accordingly.” (_Tārīkh Dimashq_)

On another occasion, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was asked about Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and replied, “Allāh named him al-Ṣiddīq upon the tongue of
Jibrīl and the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. He was the deputy of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
was pleased with him regarding our religion, so we were pleased with him
regarding our worldly affairs.” (_Ḥilyat al-Awliyāʾ_)

Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥanafīyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I asked my father, ʿAlī ibn
Abī Ṭālib, ‘Who are the best people after the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}?’ He replied, ‘Abū Bakr.’ I asked, ‘Then who?’ He replied,
‘Then ʿUmar.’ I feared he would say ʿUthmān, so I asked, ‘Then you?’ He replied,
‘I am only a man from among the Muslims.’” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

Likewise, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} famously said, “The best of this Ummah after
its Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} are Abū Bakr and ʿUmar.” (_Musnad Aḥmad_) Imam
al-Dhahabī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} commented, “This is _mutawātir_ from ʿAlī. May
Allāh curse the Rāfiḍah. How ignorant they are.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

In another narration, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Whoever prefers me over
Abū Bakr and ʿUmar, I will subject him to the punishment of the slanderer.”

Here is a family tree of the household of ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} showing the
immense love and respect they had for Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. If
anyone deserves to be called the Shīʿah of ʿAlī or the Shīʿah of Ahl al-Bayt,
then without a doubt it is Ahl al-Sunnah.

This love and recognition of their virtue did not end with ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Rather, it continued through the generations of the Ahl
al-Bayt after him.

ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn ibn al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} was asked, “What was the
status of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar in the eyes of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}?” He gestured towards their graves and said, “Just as their
relationship is with him right now.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

Bassām al-Ṣayrafī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I asked Abū Jaʿfar Muḥammad
al-Bāqir \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} about Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}.” He
replied, “By Allāh, I associate myself with them and I seek forgiveness for
them. I have not met anyone from my household who dissociates himself from
them.” (_Al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrá_)

Imam Muḥammad al-Bāqir \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} used to say, “The children of Fāṭimah
unanimously agreed to speak well of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

ʿUrwah ibn ʿAbdullāh \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} narrated, “I asked Muḥammad al-Bāqir
about decorating a sword. He replied, ‘There is no harm in it. Abū Bakr
al-Ṣiddīq decorated his sword.’ I said, ‘Do you call him al-Ṣiddīq?’ Thereupon
he immediately stood up, faced the Qiblah, and said, ‘Yes, al-Ṣiddīq! Yes,
al-Ṣiddīq! Whoever does not call him al-Ṣiddīq, may Allāh never validate any of
his statements in this world or the Hereafter.’” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

He also said, “Whoever does not recognize the virtue of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar is
ignorant of the Sunnah.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

ʿAbdullāh ibn Jaʿfar ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, who lived through the
Khilāfahs of both Abū Bakr and ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, said, “We were ruled by
Abū Bakr, the best of the Khulafāʾ, the most merciful towards us, and the
kindest to us.” (_Sharḥ Uṣūl Iʿtiqād Ahl al-Sunnah_)

Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Allāh is free from the one who
dissociates himself from Abū Bakr and ʿUmar. May Allāh be pleased with them
both.” (_Faḍāʾil al-Ṣaḥābah_) He also said, “Some of the envious zealots of Iraq
claim that we speak ill of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar. How can that ever be? They are my
fathers!” (_Sharḥ al-Sunnah_)

Imam al-Ājurrī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} records that Sālim ibn Abī Ḥafṣah said, “I
asked Abū Jaʿfar Muḥammad al-Bāqir and Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq \hon{\M_TWO}{\BODY} about
Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. They replied, ‘O Sālim, befriend them
and dissociate yourself from their enemies, for indeed they were Imams of
guidance.’”

Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} then said, “O Sālim, Abū Bakr is my
grandfather. Can a man abuse his own grandfather? May the intercession of
Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} not reach me on the Day of Judgment if I do not
befriend them and dissociate myself from their enemies.” (_Kitāb al-Sharīʿah_)

Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} would proudly say, “Abū Bakr fathered me
twice.” (_ʿUmdat al-Ṭālib_)

This statement becomes clear when one examines his lineage:

- Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq was Jaʿfar ibn Muḥammad al-Bāqir ibn ʿAlī Zayn al-ʿĀbidīn ibn
  al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}.
- His mother was Umm Farwah bint al-Qāsim ibn Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr. Through
  this line, he was a direct descendant of Muḥammad ibn Abī Bakr
  \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.
- Umm Farwah's mother was Asmāʾ bint ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn Abī Bakr
  \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}. Through this line, he was also a descendant of ʿAbd
  al-Raḥmān ibn Abī Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.
- Thus, Jaʿfar al-Ṣādiq \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} was connected to Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq
  \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} through both his maternal grandfather's line and his
  maternal grandmother's line.

Imam Abū Ḥanīfah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “I met Abū Jaʿfar Muḥammad ibn ʿAlī
and asked him about Abū Bakr and ʿUmar. He replied, ‘May Allāh have mercy upon
Abū Bakr and ʿUmar.’ I said, ‘It is said in Iraq that you dissociate yourself
from them.’ He replied, ‘Allāh forbid! Whoever says that about me is a liar. Do
you not know that ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} married his daughter
Umm Kulthūm, whose mother was Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, the daughter of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, to ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}?’” (al-Muntaẓam)

Zayd ibn ʿAlī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “Abū Bakr was the leader of the grateful
ones.” Thereafter, he recited the statement of Allāh,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَسَيَجۡزِي ٱللَّهُ ٱلشَّٰكِرِينَ ١٤٤
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
“And Allāh will reward the grateful.” (Āl ʿImrān, Q3:144)
\AYAH_END

Then he said, “To dissociate oneself from Abū Bakr is to dissociate oneself from
ʿAlī.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

Ibn ʿAbbās \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} narrated,

> When ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was placed upon his deathbed, the people
> gathered around him supplicating for him before his body was taken away. I was
> among them. Suddenly I felt someone grasp my shoulder and found that it was
> ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

> ʿAlī invoked Allāh's mercy upon ʿUmar and said, “O ʿUmar! You have not left
> behind anyone whose deeds I would more love to imitate and with whom I would
> more love to meet Allāh than you. By Allāh, I always thought that Allāh would
> place you with your two companions, for I frequently heard the Messenger of
> Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saying, ‘I, Abū Bakr and ʿUmar went somewhere; I, Abū
> Bakr and ʿUmar entered somewhere; and I, Abū Bakr and ʿUmar left somewhere.’”
> (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

Likewise, at the martyrdom of ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, it is reported that
al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} said, “Any household that does not feel
the loss of ʿUmar is indeed an evil household.” (_Al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrá_)

These statements leave little room for doubt. The household of ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, his children, grandchildren, and descendants openly praised
Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, recognized their virtue, sought Allāh's
mercy for them, and regarded them as Imams of guidance. The claim that the Ahl
al-Bayt hated or dissociated themselves from the Shaykhayn is contradicted by
the testimony of the Ahl al-Bayt themselves.

The historical record becomes even more remarkable when we examine the
relationship from the other direction. Not only did the Ahl al-Bayt love and
honor Abū Bakr and ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, but Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} likewise demonstrated immense love, reverence, and service
towards the household of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

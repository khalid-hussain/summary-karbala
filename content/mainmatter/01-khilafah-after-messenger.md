
# The Khilāfah After the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\HEADER}

<!-- TODO: Rightly Guided Caliphs OR al-Khulafāʾ al-Rāshidūn -->

<!-- TODO: Abdul Hakeem: Ibn Khadun's comment on why Mu3awiyah appointed Yazid; otherwise, it would not be accepted by the Shamis and there would be bloodshed. -->

Before discussing the events that unfolded after the era of the al-Khulafāʾ
al-Rāshidūn, it is important to understand the status of the Khulafāʾ who
succeeded the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} not only informed his Ummah that differences and trials would
arise after his passing, but he also directed them towards the path that would
safeguard them from misguidance. Al-ʿIrbāḍ ibn Sāriyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
narrated that the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,

<!-- TODO: Check translation -->

> I admonish you to fear Allāh, to listen and obey even if an Abyssinian slave is
> appointed as your leader. Because whosoever among you shall live after me will
> see much discord. So hold fast to my Sunnah and the examples of the Rightly
> Guided Khulafāʾ who will come after me. Adhere to them and hold fast to them.
> Beware of newly introduced matters (in the religion), for every innovation is
> misguidance. (Sunan Abī Dāwūd)

This tremendous narration establishes the lofty rank of the al-Khulafāʾ
al-Rāshidūn and demonstrates that adherence to their guidance is, in reality,
adherence to the guidance of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself.

The Companions \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} understood that leadership after the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was not a hereditary institution restricted to his family.
Rather, it was a matter to be determined by the Muslims through consultation and
consensus. During the final illness of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAbbās \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} narrated that ʿAlī
ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} emerged from the house of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} while the people were waiting outside. They asked him about
the condition of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, and he replied that
he had improved by the grace of Allāh. At that point, al-ʿAbbās ibn ʿAbd
al-Muṭṭalib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} took ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} by the hand and
said, "By Allāh, within three days you will be under the authority of another
man. I believe that the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} will pass away
from this illness, for I recognize the signs of death among the descendants of
ʿAbd al-Muṭṭalib. Let us go to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and ask
him regarding this matter of leadership. If it belongs to us, we will know it,
and if it belongs to others, he can advise them to treat us well." ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, "By Allāh, if we ask the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for it and he refuses us, then the people will never grant it
to us thereafter. By Allāh, I will not ask the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} for it." (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

This narration is particularly significant because it shows that ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} himself did not regard the Khilāfah as a hereditary right
reserved exclusively for Ahl al-Bayt. Had he believed leadership was divinely
restricted to his family, there would have been no reason to hesitate in seeking
explicit confirmation from the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Instead,
he recognized that this matter would be determined after the Prophet's passing
and declined to seek any special designation for himself or his household.

There are also narrations indicating that the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
directed the Companions toward Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} as the
most deserving person to assume leadership after him. A woman once approached
the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} regarding a matter, and he instructed
her to return at a later time. She said, "O Messenger of Allāh, what if I come
and do not find you?" alluding to the possibility of his death. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, "If you do not find me, then go to Abū Bakr." (Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī)

Likewise, Anas ibn Mālik \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported that Banū al-Muṣṭaliq sent
him to ask the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} regarding whom they should
give their Zakāh to after his passing. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied,
"Give it to Abū Bakr." (_Al-Mustadrak ʿalā al-Ṣaḥīḥayn_ of al-Ḥākim)

The dreams of the Prophets are a form of revelation, and among the dreams seen
by the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was one that the scholars
understood to be a clear indication of the Khilāfahs of Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "I saw (in a dream)
that the people had gathered. Then Abū Bakr stood up and pulled out one or two
buckets full of water (from a well), and there was weakness in his pulling---may
Allāh forgive him. Then Ibn al-Khaṭṭāb stood up, and the bucket turned into a
very large one, and I have never seen any strong man among the people doing such
a hard job. He pulled out so much water that the people drank to their
satisfaction and watered their camels to their fill." (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

The scholars explained that the brief drawing of water by Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} symbolized his short Khilāfah, while the tremendous amount
of water drawn by ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} symbolised the length, strength, and
vast achievements of his rule.

During the final illness of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, several
incidents occurred that further pointed towards the leadership of Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} narrated that while she was
suffering from a headache, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “I felt like
sending for Abū Bakr and his son and appointing him as my successor lest some
people claim something or some others wish something. But then I said to myself,
‘Allāh would not allow it to be otherwise, and the Muslims would prevent it from
being otherwise.’” (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

In another narration, ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} reported, “Allāh\'s Messenger
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said during his final illness, ‘Call Abū Bakr and your
brother so that I may write a document, for I fear that someone may aspire to it
and say, “I am more deserving of it.” But Allāh and the believers will accept none
but Abū Bakr.’” (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

Perhaps the clearest practical indication came during the Prophet\'s final
illness when he repeatedly instructed Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} to lead the
Muslims in prayer. ʿAbdullāh ibn Zamʿah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated that when
the illness of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} became severe, Bilāl
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} called for the prayer. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,
"Tell someone to lead the people in prayer." When ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
began leading the people, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} heard his
voice and said, "Where is Abū Bakr? Allāh does not allow that, and the Muslims
too. Allāh does not allow that, and the Muslims too." Thereafter Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was brought and led the people in prayer (Sunan Abī Dāwūd).

On another occasion, Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was leading the prayer when
the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} came out during his illness. Abū Bakr
attempted to move back, but the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} signalled for him to
remain where he was. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then sat beside
him, and the people continued following Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} in prayer
while Abū Bakr followed the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} (Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī).

Following the passing of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the Anṣār
gathered at Saqīfah Banī Sāʿidah[^saqifah] with Saʿd ibn ʿUbādah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Some suggested that there should be one leader from the
Anṣār and one from the Muhājirūn. Upon hearing this, Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, and Abū
ʿUbaydah ibn al-Jarrāḥ \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} went to meet them. ʿUmar
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} later recalled that he intended to speak, but Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} stopped him. He later said, "By Allāh, I only wished to say
something that had impressed me, but I feared that Abū Bakr would not express it
as well."

[^saqifah]: Saqīfah Banī Sāʿidah was a covered meeting place (a portico or
    pavilion) belonging to the clan of Banū Sāʿidah in Madīnah.

Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} then addressed the gathering and explained that the
Quraysh were the most recognized and respected tribe among the Arabs and that
the leadership should remain among them, while the Anṣār would remain their
ministers and advisers. When al-Ḥubāb ibn al-Mundhir \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
suggested appointing one leader from each group, Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
replied, “No. We will be the rulers, and you will be the ministers.”

He then proposed either ʿUmar or Abū ʿUbaydah \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. However,
ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} immediately took the hand of Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and said, "No, by Allāh. Rather we pledge allegiance to you,
for you are our leader, the best among us, and the most beloved of us to the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}." The people then pledged allegiance to
Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Imam Mūsá ibn ʿUqbah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} narrates that after assuming
leadership, Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} addressed the people, saying, "By
Allāh, I never desired leadership for even a day or a night. I never asked Allāh
for it openly or secretly. Rather, I feared dissension. I have been burdened
with a tremendous responsibility for which I have no strength except through
Allāh."

At that time, ʿAlī and al-Zubayr \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} said, "We were not angry
except that we were not present during the consultation, and we see Abū Bakr as
the most deserving of it. He was the Companion of the Cave. We know his virtue
and excellence. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} ordered him to lead
the people in prayer while he was still alive." (_Al-Maghāzī_ of Mūsá ibn ʿUqbah)

Similarly, Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated that Abū Bakr
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} later summoned al-Zubayr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and asked him,
"Do you wish to break the stick of the Muslims?" Al-Zubayr replied, "There is no
blame upon you, O Khalīfah of the Messenger of Allāh." He then pledged
allegiance to Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
subsequently summoned ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and addressed him similarly. ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, "There is no blame upon you, O Khalīfah of the
Messenger of Allāh." He then pledged allegiance as well. (_Al-Bidāyah wa
al-Nihāyah_)

<!-- TODO: badanah and badarah -->

This narration was considered immensely valuable by the scholars of Ḥadīth. Imam
Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} is reported to have taken this
narration to his teacher Imam Ibn Khuzaymah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}. After reviewing
it, Imam Muslim remarked that the narration was worth a _badanah_, meaning a
valuable sacrificial animal. Ibn Khuzaymah replied that it was worth far more
than that; rather, it was like a _badarah_, a bag containing thousands of gold
coins, highlighting the immense importance of this narration in clarifying the
relationship between the Companions after the passing of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The Companions \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} were human beings with emotions, feelings,
and personal opinions. Being both the cousin and son-in-law of the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, it is not difficult to understand that the thought of
assuming leadership after the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} may have crossed the
mind of ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. This is apparent from the narration mentioned
earlier regarding the final illness of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. However, what distinguished the Companions
\hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} was their ability to place the unity of Islam above personal
considerations.

Several weeks later, ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} requested a private audience with
Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} narrated, “ʿAlī said
to Abū Bakr, ‘We recognize your virtue and the favor Allāh has bestowed upon
you. We do not envy you for the good Allāh has granted you. However, you did not
consult us regarding this matter, and we believed that we had some right to be
consulted because of our close relationship to the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.’”

When Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} heard these words, he wept and replied, "By
Him in Whose Hand is my soul, maintaining ties with the family of the Messenger
of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} is more beloved to me than maintaining ties with my
own family."

He further explained that he would continue administering the Prophet\'s affairs
and property exactly as the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had done.
Thereupon ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} informed him that he would publicly renew his
pledge of allegiance. After the Ẓuhr prayer, Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
addressed the people and explained the situation. ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} then
stood, praised Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, acknowledged his right and virtue,
and clarified that his delay had not been due to jealousy or rejection of Abū
Bakr\'s leadership. The Muslims were overjoyed and said, "You have done the
right thing." Thereafter the relationship between the Muslims was restored
completely and unity prevailed. (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

These narrations, together with numerous others, demonstrate the special rank of
Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} among the Companions and help explain why
the Muhājirūn and Anṣār united upon his leadership immediately after the passing
of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Saʿīd ibn Jumhān narrated that Safīnah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The Khilāfah will remain in my
Ummah for thirty years, then there will be monarchy after that."

After narrating this _ḥadīth_, Safīnah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Count the
Khilāfah of Abū Bakr, then count the Khilāfah of ʿUmar, then count the Khilāfah
of ʿUthmān, then count the Khilāfah of ʿAlī." When the years of their respective
Khilāfahs were added together, they amounted to approximately thirty years.
(_Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī_)

For this reason, the scholars of Ahl al-Sunnah refer to Abū Bakr, ʿUmar,
ʿUthmān, and ʿAlī \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} as the al-Khulafāʾ al-Rāshidūn
(Rightly-Guided Caliphs). However, when discussing the completion of the
thirty-year period specifically mentioned by the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, it is important to note that the prophecy is only completed
with the inclusion of the six-month Khilāfah of al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. Some later writers have referred to Muʿāwiyah ibn Abī
Sufyān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} or ʿUmar ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} as the
"Fifth Rightly-Guided Caliph" due to their justice and adherence to the Sunnah.
While both were undoubtedly among the greatest rulers of Islamic history, the
fifth Rightly-Guided Caliph referred to in connection with the thirty-year
prophetic period is al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} (_Tārīkh
al-Khulafāʾ_).

After assuming the Khilāfah following the martyrdom of his father ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} in Ramaḍān 40 AH, al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} recognized
the dangers of continued conflict and the immense bloodshed that could result
from further infighting between the Muslims. Accordingly, he entered into
negotiations with Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and ultimately relinquished the
office of Khilāfah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal 41 AH in order to preserve the unity of the
Ummah. It is reported that he addressed the people saying,

> O Muslims! To me, mischief is highly detestable. I made peace with Muʿāwiyah to
> save the Ummah of my grandfather from tribulations and disorder and accepted him
> as commander and Khalīfah. Had the command and Khilāfah been his right, he has
> received it; and if it was mine, then I have bestowed it upon him.

Through this noble act, al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} fulfilled the prophecy of
the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. Abū Bakrah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
narrated, “The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was once sitting upon the pulpit with
al-Ḥasan beside him. He would sometimes look towards the people and sometimes
towards al-Ḥasan and say, ‘Indeed, this son of mine is a leader (_sayyid_), and
perhaps Allāh will reconcile through him two great groups of Muslims.’” (_Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī_)

Thus, al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} fulfilled two remarkable prophecies of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}: the prophecy that he would bring
reconciliation between two great groups of Muslims, and the prophecy that the
Khilāfah upon the methodology of Prophethood would endure for thirty years.

The duration of the Khilāfahs of the al-Khulafāʾ al-Rāshidūn is traditionally
calculated as follows:

Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}: 2 years, 3 months and 9 days (13 Rabīʿ
al-Awwal 11 AH -- 22 Jumādá al-Ākhirah 13 AH).

ʿUmar al-Fārūq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}: 10 years, 6 months and 3 days (23 Jumādá
al-Ākhirah 13 AH -- 26 Dhū al-Ḥijjah 23 AH).

ʿUthmān Dhū al-Nūrayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}: 12 years and 12 days (1 Muḥarram 24
AH -- 18 Dhū al-Ḥijjah 35 AH).

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}: 4 years and 9 months (19 Dhū al-Ḥijjah
35 AH -- 19 Ramaḍān 40 AH).

Together, these Khilāfahs amount to approximately 29 years, 6 months and 4
days.

Al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}: 6 months (19 Ramaḍān 40 AH -- 19 Rabīʿ
al-Awwal 41 AH).

With the addition of the Khilāfah of al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, the total
reaches thirty years, precisely as foretold by the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

Imam Ibn Kathīr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} writes, “Al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
relinquished the Khilāfah in Rabīʿ al-Awwal of 41 AH, thereby completing the
thirty years mentioned by the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.”
(_Al-Bidāyah wa al-Nihāyah_)


# Cursing the Ṣaḥābah (Tabarrā)

These days of Muḥarram are marked by the cursing of the Ṣaḥābah and the
spreading of lies against the Companions of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. The primary targets are our mother ʿĀʾishah, Abū Bakr
al-Ṣiddīq, and ʿUmar al-Fārūq \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}. The status of our mother
ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} has been cemented by Allāh in the Quran and will
continue to be recited until the Day of Judgement. Imam Aḥmad
\hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} stated that anyone who insults Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, or ʿĀʾishah
\hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} is not considered a Muslim. How can the accursed Shia curse
and blame ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} for Karbalāʾ when she had passed away
four years before the incident itself?

Overall, Allāh says in the Quran:

[وَالسَّابِقُونَ الْأَوَّلُونَ مِنَ الْمُهَاجِرِينَ وَالْأَنْصَارِ وَالَّذِينَ
اتَّبَعُوهُم بِإِحْسَانٍ رَّضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُمْ وَرَضُوا عَنْهُ وَأَعَدَّ
لَهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ تَجْرِي تَحْتَهَا الْأَنْهَارُ خَالِدِينَ فِيهَا أَبَدًا ۚ
ذَٰلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيم]{dir="rtl"}ُ

"And the first forerunners among the Muhājirūn and the Anṣār, and those who
followed them in excellence---Allāh is pleased with them, and they are pleased
with Him. He has prepared for them Gardens beneath which rivers flow, wherein
they shall abide forever. That is the supreme success." (Sūrat al-Tawbah, 9:100)

Imam al-Ṭaḥāwī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said in his foundational work on the creed of
Ahl al-Sunnah, \"We love the Companions of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. We do not go to excess regarding the love of any one of
them, nor do we disavow any one of them. We hate those who hate them and those
who mention them with anything but good. We mention them only with goodness.
Love for them is religion (dīn), faith (īmān), and excellence (iḥsān), while
hatred of them is disbelief (kufr), hypocrisy (nifāq), and transgression
(ṭughyān).

We affirm the Caliphate after the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} first
for Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, by virtue of his excellence and
precedence over the entire Ummah; then for ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}; then for ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}; then for ʿAlī ibn Abī
Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. They are the Rightly Guided Caliphs (al-Khulafāʾ
al-Rāshidūn) and the rightly guided Imams (al-Aʾimmah al-Muhtadūn).

We bear witness that the ten individuals whom the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} named and gave glad tidings of Paradise are indeed among the
people of Paradise, just as the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} testified
for them, and his testimony is the truth. They are: Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, ʿUthmān,
ʿAlī, Ṭalḥah, al-Zubayr, Saʿd, Saʿīd, ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf, and Abū ʿUbaydah
ibn al-Jarrāḥ, the trustworthy one (amīn) of this Ummah. May Allāh be pleased
with them all.

Whoever speaks well of the Companions of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, his wives who are pure from every defilement, and his
progeny who are purified from every impurity, has absolved himself of hypocrisy.

The scholars of the Salaf from among the earliest generations of this Ummah, and
those who followed them from the Tābiʿūn, the people of goodness and transmitted
reports (āthār), the people of fiqh and reflection, are not to be mentioned
except in a good manner. Whoever mentions them with evil is upon other than the
correct path. We do not prefer any of the Awliyāʾ over any of the Prophets
[عليهم السلام]{dir="rtl"}. Rather, we say that a single Prophet is superior to
all of the Awliyāʾ combined.\" (al-ʿAqīdah al-Ṭaḥāwiyyah)

Cursing and speaking ill of the Ṣaḥābah is, in reality, an attack upon Allāh and
His Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. It implies that the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
was not wise enough to surround himself with the right people, or that he was
unable to nurture, educate, and rectify them. It further implies that Allāh
allowed His Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to be surrounded by such people and
then allowed the religion to be preserved and transmitted through them. ʿAjīb!

Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated that the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Do not abuse my Companions, for if one of you were to
spend gold equal to Mount Uḥud in the cause of Allāh, it would not equal a mudd,
or even half a mudd, spent by one of them." (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

One mudd is approximately two handfuls of food, roughly between 510 and 750
grams. Such is the monumental and immense status of the Companions of the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. If this is what the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said regarding his Companions in general, then what about:
the people of Badr, Muʿādh ibn Jabal \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, at whose death the
Throne of Allāh shook, the ten promised Paradise, and the wives of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The esteemed Shaykh Dr. ʿAbd al-Karīm al-Khuḍayr [حفظه الله]{dir="rtl"} said,
"The background of this ḥadīth is that an altercation once took place between
Khālid ibn al-Walīd and ʿAbd al-Raḥmān ibn ʿAwf \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. During the
dispute, Khālid spoke harshly against ʿAbd al-Raḥmān. Thereupon the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said while addressing Khālid: 'Do not abuse my
Companions.'

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said this to Khālid ibn al-Walīd, and Khālid was a man
through whom Allāh aided Islam. This demonstrates the tremendous station and
status of all the Companions. If the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} became upset and
reprimanded a Companion when harsh words occurred between Companions themselves,
then what can be said about a person who is not a Companion and who curses them
while possessing no comparable weight in Islam?"

In another narration, Ibn ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} reported that the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The curse of Allāh is upon the one who insults my
Companions." (al-Muʿjam al-Awsaṭ)

Al-Barāʾ \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated, "I heard the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
say: 'The Anṣār---none loves them except a believer, and none hates them except
a hypocrite. Whoever loves them, Allāh will love him, and whoever hates them,
Allāh will hate him.'" (Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī)

Imam al-Awzāʿī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whoever curses Abū Bakr has
apostatised from his religion and his blood becomes lawful." (al-Ibānah
al-Ṣughrā) Why did he make such a strong statement? Because anyone who curses
Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} has rejected the Quran and opposed its clear
testimony.

Imam Abū Zurʿah al-Rāzī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "If you see a man criticising
one of the Companions of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, then know
that he is a heretic."

Imam Aḥmad ibn Ḥanbal \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "If you see a man mentioning one
of the Companions of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} in an evil
manner, then call his Islām into question."

Imam Mālik \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, these people wished to defame the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, but they were unable to do so, so they attacked and
defamed his Companions." (al-Ṣārim al-Maslūl)

Imam al-Dhahabī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} writes, "The person who speaks ill of or
curses the Companions of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} is deemed to have
departed from Islām and separated himself from the body of the Muslims. If he
seeks to attribute faults to the Companions of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY},
then he is considered a hypocrite." (al-Kabāʾir)

ʿĀṣim al-Aḥwal narrates that Abū al-ʿĀliyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said while
explaining the verse:

[اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيم]{dir="rtl"}َ

"Guide us to the Straight Path."

He said, "It is the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and his two Companions, Abū Bakr
and ʿUmar."

ʿĀṣim said, "I mentioned this to al-Ḥasan al-Baṣrī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} and he
replied: 'Abū al-ʿĀliyah spoke the truth and gave sincere advice.'" (al-Sunnah
of al-Marwazī)

Abū al-ʿĀliyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} was a senior Tābiʿī who was born during the
lifetime of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} but embraced Islām during
the caliphate of Abū Bakr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. He was regarded as one of the
most knowledgeable people concerning the Quran after the Ṣaḥābah. (Siyar Aʿlām
al-Nubalāʾ)

In reality, attacking the Ṣaḥābah is one of the primary means by which people
attempt to attack the Sunnah itself. If the Ṣaḥābah fall, then the Sunnah falls,
for the Quran, the Sunnah, and the religion itself have only reached us through
their sacrifices, service, and transmission.

It is sufficient for every Muslim to know that Allāh, the All-Knowing,
explicitly informed us in the Quran that He is pleased with the Ṣaḥābah. Whoever
seeks guidance should therefore be pleased with those whom Allāh Himself
declared His pleasure with.

Yet one of the most common sectarian narratives surrounding Karbalāʾ is the
claim that the Ahl al-Bayt themselves opposed and detested Abū Bakr and ʿUmar
\hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}. When we turn to the statements of the household of the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, however, we find precisely the opposite. Let the Ahl
al-Bayt speak for themselves.

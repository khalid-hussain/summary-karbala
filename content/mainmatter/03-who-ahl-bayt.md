
# Who Are the Ahl al-Bayt?

<!-- TODO: Ahl al-Bayt or People of the Household -->

The Ahl al-Bayt comprise the wives of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, his
children and descendants, the believing members of his family, and those from
Banū Hāshim and Banū al-Muṭṭalib upon whom Zakāh is prohibited. Loving the Ahl
al-Bayt is part of faith and from the foundations of Ahl al-Sunnah wa
al-Jamāʿah.

Zayd ibn Arqam \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated in a lengthy _ḥadīth_, “One day, the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} stood amongst us to address us at an oasis
called Khumm between Makkah and Madīnah. The Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} praised and glorified Allāh, then advised and reminded us.
Thereafter he said, ‘O people! I am but a human being. Soon a messenger from my
Lord will come to me and I shall answer him. I leave amongst you two weighty
matters. The first of them is the Book of Allāh in which there is guidance and
light, so hold firmly to it.’

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then strongly encouraged adherence to
the Quran. Thereafter he said, ‘My Ahl al-Bayt. I remind you of Allāh regarding
my Ahl al-Bayt. I remind you of Allāh regarding my Ahl al-Bayt. I remind you of
Allāh regarding my Ahl al-Bayt.’”

One of the narrators, Ḥuṣayn ibn Sabrah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}, asked Zayd
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, “Who are the Ahl al-Bayt? Are not the wives of the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} from the Ahl al-Bayt?”

Zayd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, “Yes. His wives are from the Ahl al-Bayt.
However, the Ahl al-Bayt are those amongst whom Zakāh is not permissible.”

Ḥuṣayn \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} asked, “Who are they?” Zayd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
replied, “They are the family of ʿAlī, the family of ʿAqīl, the family of
Jaʿfar, and the family of al-ʿAbbās.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim_)

This narration explicitly establishes that the wives of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} are from the Ahl al-Bayt, whilst also highlighting the wider
family members upon whom Zakāh is prohibited.

Likewise, the Ṣaḥābah \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY} once asked, “O Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, how should we send salutations upon you?” The Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, “O Allāh, send Your blessings upon Muḥammad,
his wives, and his progeny, just as You sent blessings upon the family of
Ibrāhīm. And send Your blessings upon Muḥammad, his wives, and his progeny, just
as You sent blessings upon the family of Ibrāhīm. Indeed, You are the Most
Praiseworthy, the Most Glorious.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

Allāh Himself referred to the wives of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} as part of
the Ahl al-Bayt when He said,

وَقَرۡنَ فِي بُيُوتِكُنَّ وَلَا تَبَرَّجۡنَ تَبَرُّجَ ٱلۡجَٰهِلِيَّةِ ٱلۡأُولَىٰۖ وَأَقِمۡنَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَءَاتِينَ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَأَطِعۡنَ ٱللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُۥٓۚ إِنَّمَا يُرِيدُ ٱللَّهُ لِيُذۡهِبَ عَنكُمُ ٱلرِّجۡسَ أَهۡلَ ٱلۡبَيۡتِ وَيُطَهِّرَكُمۡ تَطۡهِيرٗا ٣٣
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Remain in your homes and do not display yourselves as women did in the days of
former ignorance. Establish prayer, give Zakāh, and obey Allāh and His
Messenger. Allāh only intends to remove impurity from you, O People of the
Household, and to purify you completely. (Sūrah al-Aḥzāb, Q33:33)
\AYAH_END

There are likewise other verses in the Quran wherein the wives of Prophet
Ibrāhīm \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} and Prophet Mūsá \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} are referred to
as part of their respective Ahl.

After the wives of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the highest rank amongst the
Ahl al-Bayt belongs to his children and grandchildren. ʿĀʾishah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} narrated, “The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} came out one
morning wearing a striped cloak made from black camel hair. Then al-Ḥasan ibn
ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} came and he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} placed him beneath it.
Then al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} came and entered beneath it. Then Fāṭimah
\hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} came and he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} placed her beneath it. Then
ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} came and he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} placed him
beneath it. Then he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} recited, ‘Allāh only intends to remove
impurity from you, O People of the Household, and to purify you completely.’”
(_Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim_)

This _ḥadīth_ highlights the special virtue of ʿAlī, Fāṭimah, al-Ḥasan, and
al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}, whilst not excluding the wives of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} from the designation of Ahl al-Bayt, as the Quran had already
established their inclusion.

The only non-Arab to be granted a special connection to the Ahl al-Bayt was
Salmān al-Fārisī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
said regarding him, “He possessed knowledge of the former scriptures and
knowledge of the latter revelation. He is an ocean whose depths cannot be
reached. He is from us, the People of the Household.” (_Al-Ṭabaqāt al-Kubrá_)
Because of this special honor, Salmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} would not accept
charity for the remainder of his life.

Ahl al-Sunnah therefore love all of the Ahl al-Bayt: the wives of the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, his children, his grandchildren, and his believing
relatives. We neither neglect their rights nor exaggerate their status beyond
what Allāh and His Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} granted them. Loving the Ahl
al-Bayt is religion, honoring them is worship, and following their example is
guidance.


# Attributing Divinity to al-Ḥusayn

It is important to clarify that the term Shīʿat ʿAlī ("the party" or "supporters
of ʿAlī") did exist during the first century of Islam. At that time, however, it
referred primarily to a political affiliation rather than a distinct theological
sect. The term was used for those who supported the Khilāfah and political cause
of ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} during the civil conflicts that
followed the martyrdom of ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. They did not hold the
later doctrines associated with Twelver Shīʿism, such as the infallibility of
the Imams, the alleged alteration of the Quran, the doctrine of occultation, or
the cursing of the majority of the Ṣaḥābah. In creed and worship, the early
Shīʿat ʿAlī were mainstream Muslims who shared the same fundamental beliefs and
religious foundations as the rest of the Ummah.

The theological doctrines of Shiism developed gradually over the following
centuries. Extremist groups (_ghulāt_) began appearing during the late second
century, particularly around figures such as al-Mukhtār al-Thaqafī and later
sectarian movements. By the second and third centuries, Kūfah had become a major
center for the development of Shīʿī theology, while the city of Qum in Persia
emerged as one of its most important strongholds. It was there that many of the
doctrines now associated with Twelver Shiism were systematized, transmitted,
and preserved. The major Twelver _ḥadīth_ collections themselves were compiled
during this later period, long after the era of ʿAlī, al-Ḥasan, and al-Ḥusayn
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

For this reason, it is historically inaccurate to project the beliefs of modern
Twelver Shiism back onto the household of ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} or the early
political supporters of the Ahl al-Bayt. The sources show that the earliest
generations of the Ahl al-Bayt held many positions fundamentally at odds with
the doctrines later adopted by the Rāfiḍah.

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “Do not exaggerate my status as
the Christians exaggerated regarding the son of Maryam. Verily, I am only a
servant, so say: the servant of Allāh and His Messenger.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

Many people call upon al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} in _duʿāʾ_ and place him in
a position that belongs only to Allāh. How can we call upon al-Ḥusayn
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} when we cannot even call upon his blessed grandfather, the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}? Al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} is free
from all of this. If anyone deserves to be called the Shīʿah of ʿAlī, the Shīʿah
of al-Ḥusayn, or the Shīʿah of Ahl al-Bayt, then without a doubt it is Ahl
al-Sunnah. The word _shīʿah_ simply means a group or a party.

When al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} left Madīnah, he placed his _tawakkul_ in
Allāh alone. When he was in Makkah, he relied upon Allāh alone. When he
journeyed towards Kūfah, he invoked Allāh alone for assistance against the
tyrants. When he stood at Karbalāʾ facing his enemies, he supplicated to Allāh
alone for strength, patience, and steadfastness. When members of his family were
martyred, he praised Allāh for their sacrifice. When he entered the battlefield,
he sought help from Allāh alone.

Al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} left behind a lesson for this Ummah: that a person
of Tawḥīd prostrates only to Allāh and calls only upon Allāh, regardless of the
circumstances he faces.

He did not invoke anyone besides Allāh in his struggle against falsehood. There
is no authentic report from any group that al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} called
upon the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, his father ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, his
mother Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, or his brother al-Ḥasan \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
while standing upon the battlefield. Salutations upon the martyr of Karbalāʾ,
the grandson of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, the leader of the youth of
Jannah, and a preacher of Tawḥīd.

Yaḥyá ibn Saʿīd reported that ʿAlī ibn al-Ḥusayn \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “O
people of Iraq! Love us with the love of Islam, and do not love us with the love
of idols, for your love for us has reached a level that has become a source of
shame for us.” (_Siyar Aʿlām al-Nubalāʾ_)

It is also reported that some of the final words of al-Ḥusayn
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} were, “Patience upon Your decree, O my Lord. There is no
deity besides You, O Helper of those who seek help. I have no Lord besides You
and no deity worthy of worship except You. Patience upon Your judgment. O Helper
of the one who has no helper, O Everlasting One whose existence never ceases, O
Giver of life to the dead, O One who watches over every soul and what it has
earned. Judge between them and me, for You are the best of judges.”

Notice how, even in the final moments of his life, al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
directed every word of his supplication to Allāh alone.

Those who call upon al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} say, “Imam al-Ḥusayn was
helpless and defenceless when he was martyred,” yet in the same breath they say,
“Yā Ḥusayn, help me!” Let that marinate for a moment. If he had no power to help
himself and his family while alive, how can he help you after his passing?

Allāh says,

\AYAH_AR_START
لَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِٱللَّهِۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
There is no power except with Allāh. (Al-Kahf, Q18:39)
\AYAH_END

And what is it that we repeat dozens of times every day in every _rakʿah_ of our
prayer?

\AYAH_AR_START
إِيَّاكَ نَعۡبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسۡتَعِينُ ٥
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help. (Al-Fātiḥah, Q1:5)
\AYAH_END

If this was not enough, then let us compare al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} with
his own grandfather, the Messenger of Allah \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. There is no
doubt whatsoever that the station of our beloved Prophet Muḥammad
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} far surpasses that of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. 

Yet despite this lofty rank, Allāh did not grant His Messenger
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} the qualities that many people today attribute to al-Ḥusayn
and other righteous men. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was the most beloved of
creation to Allāh, the leader of the children of Ādam, the Seal of the Prophets,
and the greatest of all mankind. If such powers and attributes were not granted
to him, then how can they be attributed to anyone who came after him?

One of the most dangerous situations we encounter in the Sīrah is during the
Battle of Uḥud. The Muslims had become scattered, the pagan cavalry led by
Khālid ibn al-Walīd had managed to attack from behind, and a group of enemy
fighters advanced towards a small position on Mount Uḥud where the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had taken refuge with only a handful of Companions.

This is why our mother ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} famously asked the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, “Have you encountered a day harder than the day of Uḥud?”
(_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_) Anas \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} narrated that the Messenger of
Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} had his front teeth broken on the day of Uḥud and
suffered wounds to his forehead such that blood flowed down his blessed face. As
he wiped away the blood he said, “How can a people succeed after injuring their
Prophet?” (_Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim_)

In another narration he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “How can a people succeed after
doing this to their Prophet while he is calling them to their Lord, the Mighty
and Majestic?” (_Musnad Aḥmad_)

It was then that Allāh revealed,

\AYAH_AR_START
لَيۡسَ لَكَ مِنَ ٱلۡأَمۡرِ شَيۡءٌ أَوۡ يَتُوبَ عَلَيۡهِمۡ أَوۡ يُعَذِّبَهُمۡ فَإِنَّهُمۡ ظَٰلِمُونَ ١٢٨ وَلِلَّهِ مَا فِي ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِي ٱلۡأَرۡضِۚ يَغۡفِرُ لِمَن يَشَآءُ وَيُعَذِّبُ مَن يَشَآءُۚ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٞ رَّحِيمٞ ١٢٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
You have no say in the matter. It is up to Allāh whether He turns to them in
mercy or punishes them, for indeed they are wrongdoers. To Allāh belongs
whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. He forgives whom He
wills and punishes whom He wills. And Allāh is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful.
(Āl ʿImrān, Q3:128--9)
\AYAH_END

Why is this significant? Some of the very people responsible for harming the
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} at Uḥud would later embrace Islam. Not only would
they become Muslims, but men such as Khālid ibn al-Walīd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
would go on to become among the greatest champions of Islam and earn the title
“The Sword of Allāh” from the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself.

Allāh explicitly says,

\AYAH_AR_START
قُل لَّآ أَقُولُ لَكُمۡ عِندِي خَزَآئِنُ ٱللَّهِ وَلَآ أَعۡلَمُ ٱلۡغَيۡبَ وَلَآ أَقُولُ لَكُمۡ إِنِّي مَلَكٌۖ إِنۡ أَتَّبِعُ إِلَّا مَا يُوحَىٰٓ إِلَيَّۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Say, “I do not tell you that I possess the treasures of Allāh, nor do I know the
unseen, nor do I claim to be an angel. I only follow what has been revealed to
me.” (Al-Anʿām, Q6:50)
\AYAH_END

And He says,

\AYAH_AR_START
قُلۡ إِنِّي لَآ أَمۡلِكُ لَكُمۡ ضَرّٗا وَلَا رَشَدٗا ٢١
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Say, “It is not in my power to harm or benefit you.” (Al-Jinn, Q72:21)
\AYAH_END

Commenting on this _āyah_, Imam Ibn Kathīr \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “The
meaning is: I am only a human being like yourselves who receives revelation. I
am merely a servant from among the servants of Allāh. I have no control over
your guidance or your misguidance. Rather, all of that belongs to Allāh, the
Most High and Majestic.” (_Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr_)

Imam al-Baghawī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, “This _āyah_ was revealed when the
disbelievers demanded miraculous signs. So Allāh instructed the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to say, ‘I do not say to you that I possess the treasures of
Allāh,’ meaning the provisions and riches of Allāh which I could distribute
however I wished. ‘Nor do I know the unseen,’ meaning that I know nothing of
hidden matters except what Allāh informs me of. ‘Nor do I claim to be an angel,’
because angels possess abilities and perceptions that humans do not possess.”
(_Tafsīr al-Baghawī_)

These _āyāt_ alone are sufficient for any sincere person to understand the
pristine message of Tawḥīd that the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} came with.

During his lifetime, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} did not possess
knowledge of the unseen except what Allāh revealed to him. Had he possessed
unrestricted knowledge of the unseen, treacherous individuals would never have
been able to plot against him or his Companions.

Allāh informed him \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} that he had no control over forgiveness or
punishment, even regarding those who physically wounded him at Uḥud. If he did
not possess such authority during his lifetime, then how can anyone claim that
he possesses it after his passing?

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} did not exercise independent control over the
elements, the heavens, or the affairs of creation except in specific instances
by the permission of Allāh. Had he possessed such powers intrinsically, the
Muslims would never have suffered hunger, thirst, fear, and hardship.

Even when _salām_ is sent upon him at his blessed grave, he does not hear it
independently. Rather, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} informed us that Allāh has
appointed angels who convey the _salām_ of his Ummah to him. (_Sunan al-Nasāʾī_)

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said, “I am only a human being like you; I
forget just as you forget.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_) And he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,
“I am only a human being; I become angry just as human beings become angry.”
(_Sunan Abī Dāwūd_)

In another incident during the expedition of Tabūk, the Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} lost his camel and the hypocrites began mocking him.
Thereupon he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, “By Allāh, I know nothing except what
Allāh has taught me, and He has shown me where it is.” He then informed them of
its exact location and it was found precisely where he described. (_Sīrah Ibn
Isḥāq_)

Likewise, shortly before the Farewell Pilgrimage, the greatest of the angels,
Jibrīl \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY}, asked the greatest of mankind \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
about the Hour. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, “The one being asked
knows no more about it than the one asking.” (_Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī_)

If the Imam of Banī Ādam, the leader of all the Prophets and Messengers
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, was not granted knowledge of the unseen except what Allāh
revealed, nor authority over the affairs of creation, nor independent power to
benefit or harm, then how can such qualities be attributed to men who came
centuries after him?

How can al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} be called upon for help when al-Ḥusayn
himself called only upon Allāh? How can people seek rescue from him when he
sought rescue only from Allāh? How can people attribute to him powers that were
never granted to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself?

This is why the true love of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} is to follow his
example. He lived upon Tawḥīd, he called to Tawḥīd, and he died upon Tawḥīd. May
Allāh be pleased with him, elevate his rank, and gather us with him, his father,
his mother, his brother, and his grandfather in the highest levels of Paradise.

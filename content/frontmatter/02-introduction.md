
# Introduction {.unnumbered}

﷽

In the Name of Allāh, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. All types of
thanks and praise belong to Allāh, the most worthy of thanks and praise.
A praise superior to all other types of praise, like the superiority of
Allāh over all of His creation. All praise belongs to Allāh who bestows
understanding of the Dīn upon whom He wills from His servants and grants
success to those obedient to Him. All praise is due to Him who directed
mankind to recognize His Existence through His signs and left them no
excuse to disobey Him as He conveyed His message to humanity through His
Messengers, the best of His creation. I bear witness that nothing
deserves to be worshipped save Allāh alone, and I bear witness that
Muḥammad is His slave and Messenger. May Allāh bestow blessings upon
him, his family, and his companions as long as the clouds bring about
rain and as long as the thunder comes after the lightning.

Few events in Islamic history have been discussed, debated, mourned,
politicized, exaggerated, and distorted as much as the tragedy of
Karbalāʾ. More than fourteen centuries have passed since the martyrdom
of al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, yet the events that
unfolded on the plains of Karbalāʾ on the 10th of Muḥarram, 61 AH (680
CE), continue to influence the hearts, minds, and beliefs of millions of
Muslims across the world.

Unfortunately, the discussion surrounding Karbalāʾ is often clouded by
emotion, sectarian polemics, fabricated reports, historical
embellishments, and outright falsehoods. Entire narratives have been
constructed upon weak reports, anonymous sources, and the writings of
individuals whom the scholars of Ḥadīth declared unreliable. As a
result, many Muslims know Karbalāʾ through stories rather than sources,
through slogans rather than scholarship, and through inherited
narratives rather than verified history.

The purpose of this work is not to diminish the tragedy of Karbalāʾ, nor
to reduce the status of its martyrs. Rather, it is precisely because of
the immense rank of al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} and
the Ahl al-Bayt that their story deserves to be told accurately and
authentically. Their sacrifices are too noble to be mixed with
fabrication, and their virtues too great to require exaggeration.

The position of Ahl al-Sunnah wa al-Jamāʿah has always been one of
balance. We love the Ahl al-Bayt of the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. We honor them, defend them, invoke Allāh\'s pleasure
upon them, and recognize their immense virtue and lofty station. Loving
the family of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} is not optional; it is part of
faith itself.

If anyone deserves to be called the true Shīʿah of ʿAlī, al-Ḥasan,
al-Ḥusayn, and the Ahl al-Bayt \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}, then it is
Ahl al-Sunnah wa al-Jamāʿah. For the word _shīʿah_ originally means
nothing more than a group, party, or followers. We love the Ahl al-Bayt
as they commanded us to love them, without neglect and without
exaggeration, and exactly how they taught us.

Al-Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} was, above all else, a
preacher of Tawḥīd and a servant of Allāh. When he left Madīnah, he
placed his trust in Allāh alone. When he arrived in Makkah, he relied
upon Allāh alone. When he journeyed towards Kūfah, he sought aid from
Allāh alone. When he stood upon the plains of Karbalāʾ facing
overwhelming odds, he turned to Allāh alone for strength, patience, and
victory. When tragedy struck his family, he responded with patience and
submission to the decree of his Lord.

There exists no authentic report that al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} called upon
anyone besides Allāh during those final moments; neither his grandfather, the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, nor his father ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY},
nor his mother Fāṭimah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY}, nor his brother al-Ḥasan
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. Rather, his life and martyrdom remain among the greatest
demonstrations of sincere reliance upon Allāh and unwavering adherence to
Tawḥīd. To honor al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} is to honor the message for which
he lived, struggled, and died: worshipping Allāh alone without partner.

This book therefore approaches Karbalāʾ through the lens of authentic
historical sources and the methodology of the scholars of Ahl al-Sunnah.
It seeks to place the events in their proper context, beginning with the
final years of Muʿāwiyah ibn Abī Sufyān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, the
appointment of Yazīd, the invitations sent from Kūfah, the mission of
Muslim ibn ʿAqīl \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY}, the betrayal of the Kufans, the
journey of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and finally the events
that unfolded at Karbalāʾ itself.

However, Karbalāʾ cannot be understood in isolation from Muḥarram. One
of the greatest mistakes made in discussing this month is reducing its
significance to a single historical event. Long before Karbalāʾ, Allāh
had already made Muḥarram one of the four sacred months. Long before the
martyrdom of al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, it was a month
connected to the victory of Mūsá \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} over Firʿawn,
a month singled out by the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} as “the
Month of Allāh,” and a month in which fasting carries immense virtue.

For this reason, the second half of this work addresses many of the
practices and beliefs that have become attached to Muḥarram over the
centuries. Is the 10th of Muḥarram a day of mourning? What is the ruling
on self-flagellation, processions, and _taʿziyah_? What is the Islamic
position regarding cursing the Ṣaḥābah? What is the difference between
loving al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and elevating him beyond the
limits set by Islam? What did the scholars of Ahl al-Sunnah say
regarding Yazīd and his role in these events? These and other related
issues will be examined through the Quran, the Sunnah, and the
statements of the recognized scholars of this Ummah.

This work does not claim to be exhaustive. Entire libraries have been
written on Karbalāʾ, the Ahl al-Bayt, and the events of the first
Islamic century. Rather, the aim is to present a clear, accessible, and
authentic account for the ordinary Muslim; one that remains faithful to
the sources while avoiding both exaggeration and neglect.

We ask Allāh to make this work sincere for His sake, beneficial for its readers,
and a means of increasing love for the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY},
his blessed family, and his noble companions \hon{\P_THM}{\BODY}. May Allāh be
pleased with al-Ḥasan and al-Ḥusayn \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, the leaders of the
youth of Paradise. May He elevate the rank of the martyrs of Karbalāʾ and raise
us with them. May He unite the hearts of the Muslims upon the truth, protect us
from innovation and misguidance, and allow us to follow the path of the Quran,
the Sunnah, and the righteous generations of this Ummah.

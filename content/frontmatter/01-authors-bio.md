
# About the Author {.unnumbered}

Mohammad Zahid ibn Tariq al-Nagrāmī al-Najdī hails from a family of
scholarly heritage in India, which has produced distinguished scholars
such as Shaykh Owais al-Nagrāmī al-Nadwī, Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān
al-Nagrāmī al-Nadwī, Shaykh Idrīs al-Nagrāmī, and Shaykh Ḥāfiẓ Sayyid
ʿAbd al-ʿAlī, among many others.

He is a prolific author, editor, and translator, with multiple works
published and circulated internationally. Some of his works include:

- The Ultimate Profit: 40 Hadith on Ṣadaqah
- Lost Treasures: Reviving the Neglected Sunnahs of Our Prophet Muḥammad
  \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
- Al-Quds: The City of Jerusalem --- The Chronicles of Bani Israel
- The Journey of a Lifetime: Ḥajj & Qurbān Made Easy
- Zakāh Made Easy
- Istikhārah Made Easy
- Duʿāʾ & Dhikr Made Easy
- The Pathway to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}: A Beginner's Guide to the
  Science of Ḥadīth --- Translation of the work of Shaykh al-Tafsīr
  Owais al-Nagrāmī
- The Qurʾānic Blueprint --- Translation of the work of Shaykh al-Tafsīr
  Owais al-Nagrāmī
- Women in Islam --- Translation of the work of Shaykh ʿAbd al-Raḥmān
  al-Nagrāmī

Grounded in both classical Islamic scholarship and contemporary
professional disciplines, he holds certifications in Sharīʿah Auditing
and Advisory (CSAA), Project Management (PMP), Blockchain (KBA), Zakāt
Management (IIIBF), and Waqf Management (IIIBF). He also earned a
Master's degree in Information Technology (MIT), with a specialization
in IT Project Management, from the International Islamic University
Malaysia (IIUM).

Currently, Mohammad Zahid serves as a Product Manager at a leading
Islamic FinTech company in Malaysia, where he integrates technological
innovation with Islamic financial principles, governance, and ethics.
Beyond his professional work, he has been actively engaged in _daʿwah_ and
Islamic learning for over a decade, studying under esteemed scholars
including Shaykh Dr. Ibrahim Nuhu, as well as other scholars across
Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and India.

His commitment to Islamic education and literary excellence led him to
establish InkOfFaith.com, an influential online platform dedicated to
reviving the culture of reading and nurturing Islamic learning,
particularly among younger audiences. Mohammad Zahid is also a fellow
editor and regular contributor to Dakwah Corner Publications, where he
plays a key role in producing and curating high-quality Islamic
literature for a global readership.

Driven by a vision to bridge faith, knowledge, and innovation, he
continues to contribute meaningfully to contemporary discourse on Islam,
education, Islamic finance, and technology.
